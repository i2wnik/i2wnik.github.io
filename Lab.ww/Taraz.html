Taraz

Ancient Taraz Archaeological Park and Museum

The Museum and Archaeological Park "Ancient Taraz" was opened for the 550th anniversary of the Kazakh Khanate. 2.5 billion tenge was allocated for the construction of this complex, unique for Central Asia, with an area of ​​20 hectares. It is included in the list of 100 sacred places of Kazakhstan and claims to be included in the UNESCO World Heritage List.

In general, the architectural and urban planning concept of the "Ancient Taraz" center is based on the idea of ​​the Great Silk Road, its visitors get acquainted with the main components of the complex during a walk. This is an old necropolis (mausoleums of Karakhan, Dauytbek), trade and craft rows, “House of Friendship” on the square, in front of which there is a stele “Taraz sazy” (“Melody of Taraz”). Nearby there is a public garden “Mynbulak” with an observation tower. Further to the museum "Ancient Taraz" the visitor passes along the alley "The Great Silk Road". The museum and archaeological site rounds off the walking route. This center was opened in 2015. Authors of the project: Eskander Baytenov, Galym Isabaev.

Aisha Bibi Mausoleum

MAUSOLEUM AISHA BIBI is an architectural monument of the 11-12 centuries, located in the village of Aisha bibi, Zhambyl region, Zhambyl region, 18 km from the city of Taraz. It is an architectural monument of republican significance. Aisha Bibi was the lover of Karakhan, who built a mausoleum in her memory. The base of the mausoleum is cubic. Massive columns are placed at its four corners. Small niches, small columns, vaults, pointed arches, terracotta facing tiles were used in the design. The patterns of the mausoleum combine traditional types of ornamental art of the ancient tribes of Kazakhstan, including geometric, zoomorphic and solar motifs, rooted in the art of the Andronovo and Saka tribes.

Karahan Mausoleum

Karakhan mausoleum is an architectural monument preserved in Kazakhstan from the 11th century. On the western outskirts of the modern city of Taraz. The mausoleum is located at the head of the tomb of the famous khan Sha-Mahmud Bugra Karakhan of the Karakhan dynasty. The mausoleum, whose only dome collapsed, has four walls and a double tower decorated with shiny bricks, which were preserved until the 90s of the 19th century. In 1905, the fallen part was completely demolished and replaced by a new mausoleum in 1906. The new construction was financed by Said Bakhanov, then a Tashkent trustee. The later mausoleum is different from the first.

Although the facade is similar to the mausoleum of Aisha Bibi, the brickwork is similar to the medieval Ismail dome in Bukhara. Karakhan mausoleum consists of a rectangular, spacious middle room and three small parts. The roof of the central room is covered like a yurt. There are two towers at the back. The front of the mausoleum faces south, and the tomb inside is located from right to left. The entrance door is decorated with an arched cavity. The inner walls of the mausoleum were built with their own bricks, and new bricks were used for the exterior.
