The great Steppe

Karkaraly National Park

Karkaraly state National Natural Park is a mountain and forest landscape in Karkaraly District of Karaganda region. It was founded in 1998 to protect unique natural objects, develop travel and improve public recreation. In 2009, the territory was expanded from 90.3 thousand hectares to 112,120 hectares.

It occupies a zone of Steppe vegetation and pine forests in the karkaraly and Kent mountains of Saryarka. In this region, there used to be high mountains, which were destroyed over time and divided into many small hills and hills. Their high slopes have undergone severe changes, and the rocky peaks are separated by deep ravines and valleys. In many places, granite slabs are exposed. Streams and small rivers originate from the mountain ravines.

Ulytau mountains, Zhoshy, Alasha Khan mousolei

Ulytau, the ‘Great Mountains’, rise up from the surrounding steppe some 130km to the northwest of Zhezkazgan. This ancient granite massif is not particularly high: its highest peak, bearing the name White Mosque, reaches a height of 1,131m. But this range, located at the geographical centre of Kazakhstan, has a place at the heart of the development of the Kazakh people.

The mausoleum of Jochi Khan is an ancient architectural monument located on the Bank of the Karakengir River, 50 km north – east of Zhezkazgan. According to the symbols stored in the dome, all tribes subordinate to Jochi (Oghuz, argyn, Kipchak, Kerey, Naiman, konyrat, kangly, etc.) took part in its construction.

Near to the mausoleum of Jochi placed Alasha Khan mausoleum. Alasha Khan was not in history, he is a legend of the Kazakh people. But historians say that this is the coffin of the not found Temu Chin (Chingiz Khan).
