The mausoleum of Hodja Ahmed

The mausoleum of Hodja Ahmed Yasawi is an architectural building built in the late XIV century in Turkestan. After the death of Hodja Ahmed Yassavi, he was buried in a small mazar built for him by a large crowd. Later, the mausoleum became a place of mass pilgrimage for Muslims. The mausoleum of Hodja Ahmed Yasawi is a domed building with a large portal. It is 46.5 m wide and 65 m long. The building has a huge portal (width - about 50 meters, the length of the portal arch - 18.2 m) and several domes. There are 35 rooms for different purposes around its central hall. The height of the building to the top of the dome of the church (here it is called a boiler due to the presence of a boiler) is 37.5 meters. The thickness of the outer walls is 1.8-2 m, the thickness of the boiler walls is 3 meters. This tall building, which is symmetrical in general and asymmetrical in individual parts, consists of 8 groups of different rooms.

Arystanbab mausoleum

Arystanbab mausoleum (kaz. Arystanbab kesenesi) is a mausoleum on the grave of the teacher and spiritual mentor Khoja Ahmed Yasavi, a religious mystic and preacher Arystanbab. The mausoleum is located 150 kilometers from the city of Shymkent, not far from the city of Otrar and the village of Shaulder, Otyrar district. Architectural monument. One of the main places of worship for Muslims in the region. Since 1982, the Arystan-Bab mausoleum has been under state protection.

Karatau Nature Reserve

The Karatau State Nature Reserve is located in the central part of the Karatau ridge, which is an offshoot of the northwestern arcs of the Tien Shan on the territory of the South Kazakhstan region. The slopes of the ridge are dissected by deep valleys of numerous rivers and temporary streams with seasonal surface runoff almost everywhere.

Amphitheater

The amphitheater is located between Bayburt and Tauke Khan streets, in the central part of Turkestan, in the historical center, in close proximity to the protected area of ​​the Kozha Akhmet Yassaui Mosque, protected by UNESKO, on a land plot of 0.55 hectares. Building area - 2,241 m2. The total area of ​​the building is 3,911 m2.
